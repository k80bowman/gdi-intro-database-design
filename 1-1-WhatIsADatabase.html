What is a database?

A set of related data and the way it is organized!

What is a database?

A collection of schemas, tables, queries, reports, views, and other objects (it’s not just tables of data!) often organized in a way that supports processes or reflects real life in order to facilitate business or functions.

What is a database - what are those things?!

A collection of schemas, tables, queries, views, and other objects…

  • Schema = logical grouping of objects (tables, etc.), often in a functional way.
    • So for 1 database you may have an HR schema and a sales schema
  • Table = collection of related data arranged in columns and rows.
  • Table = collection of related data arranged in columns and rows.
  • Query = request for information from a database.
  • View = result of a stored query.
    • A view looks like a table, but a view’s data is calculated in real-time. Also provide pretty significant advantages to a table - can restrict access to just views (can be a subset of data), can simplify or aggregate data, take very little space to store since a database stores just the definition of the view, not a copy of all the data it has.
