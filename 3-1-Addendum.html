Database Careers

Database Administrator (DBA)

Takes care of the database itself!

  • Upgrades the server and application tools
  • Manages backups and restores
  • Manages database availability
  • Takes care of partitioning
  • Manages security
  • Manages performance
  • Troubleshoots


(CS education with a focus on database administration or MIS)

This is the person called when the DB is down at 4AM on a weekend. MIS = Management Information Systems

Database Developer

Software developer who specializes in databases.

  • Writes queries
  • Scripts schema changes
  • Creates stored procedures
  • Creates triggers and constraints
  • Designs the database
  • Also works on performance and security


(CS education)

If the administrator is the maintenance and protection of the database, the developer is the improvements and the functionality.

BI Analyst

Writes custom queries and reports, including data visualization.

  • May be more of a report writer or business analyst or may be more of a developer, building solutions
  • SQL language experts
  • Collects data for the business
  • May work with SSRS, Crystal Reports, Tableau/Power BI, Microsoft Access


(CS or BA education depending on focus and job role)

BI analyst may work within the DBMS if their DBMS has the desired tools included, or they may use external tool like tableau.

Database Consultant

Specialist DBAs or developers; experts in the field.

  • Tunes performance
  • Enhances security
  • Improves availability
  • Implements/migrates to cloud systems


(Lots of experience and skill in their chosen specialty)

performance = finding bottlenecks security = encryption availability = mirroring and fail-over clustering Implements features that may not be considered standard. May be more like a DBA or may be more like a developer, but either way they’re brought in to solve problems or improve the system.

Data Scientist

Specialize in creating data models.

  • Taking large amounts of data and turning it into usable insights
  • Creates predictive modeling (using statistics to predict outcomes)
  • Use dashboards with current data, as opposed to a statistician’s report


(Math and statistics with an understanding of CS)

Predictive modeling is often used in customer relationship modeling - what coupons will work on a customer, what products a customer may like. Also used in things like auto insurance to determine risk.
